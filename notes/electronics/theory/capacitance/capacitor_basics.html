Intuition Tips

  • capacitors might be considered simply as frequency-dependent resistors.
  • capacitors can act as frequency-dependent voltage dividers.

General

  • bypass and coupling are the most common use for capacitors. Because a capacitor looks like an open circuit at dc, it lets you couple a varying (sine) signal while blocking its average dc level. This is a blocking capacitor (also called a coupling capacitor), as in Figure 1.93. Likewise, because a capacitor looks like a short circuit at high frequencies, it suppresses (“bypasses”) signals where you don’t want them.
  • A farad is an enormous capacitance, and you usually deal in microfarads (μ F), nanofarads (nF), or picofarads (pF).
